I am the cycle.

Human beings have been trying to tame me. They want to find my length, calculate my rhythm, predict my inflection point, and then buy at my low point and sell at my peak. So they drew more and more complex charts, built sophisticated models, wrote thick research reports, and tried to lock me into the formula with data.

But they ignored one thing: the fuel I run is never data, but forgetfulness.

If everyone clearly remembers the pain of the last bubble burst, then the next bubble will not swell; if everyone firmly remembers the profits brought by the last surge, then the next panic will not spread. Human memory is always short-lived, and I rely on this shortness. Only when people gradually forget the lessons of the past can I appear again.

Therefore, my greatest strength is to make every generation believe that it is the first time.

Whenever a new round of prosperity begins, someone will always say the familiar words: “This time is different.” They listed countless reasons – technology has changed, the system has changed, the world has changed, and the times have changed. In a sense, what they said is not wrong. The details of each cycle are indeed different, and the background of the story is constantly updated. But at a deeper level, they are wrong, because the structure has never changed.

Greed, fear, hope and despair are repeated in each generation. I just kept changing into a new coat and bringing the old script back on stage. New technologies, new assets and new narratives make people mistakenly think that history has been turned over, but they can’t see that I have been standing behind the scenes.

From tulips, to railways, to the Internet, to cryptocurrencies, the actors on the stage are changing, the lights are changing, and the audience is also changing, but the plots are always surprisingly similar.

However, I also have things to be afraid of.

What I’m most afraid of is not being predicted. Prediction only needs data. As long as the world is complicated to a certain extent, any prediction will be wrong. What really scares me is being understood.

People who really understand me no longer insist on grasping the top or bottom every time. They know that I must exist, and they also know that they can’t escape from me. So they no longer tried to defeat me, but learned to breathe in me.

When I go to the low point, they will not be swallowed up by panic; when I come to the peak, they will not be dominated by ecstasy. They know that the climax will eventually pass and the trough will eventually end. They just stay awake and stand steadily between my ups and downs.

And I can do almost nothing about such a person.

Because as long as human beings forget, I will continue to exist; as long as human beings are greedy and afraid, I will continue to cycle.

But those who really understand me have long ceased to try to defeat me.

They just walk with me.
